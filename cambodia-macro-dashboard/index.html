Environment & Climate Watch — satellite indicators of activity, agriculture and vegetation
Filters to Hover the ⓘ on each card for what the indicator means

NO₂ Concentration — Tropospheric Column ⓘ

Monthly mean over the selected area, 10¹⁵ molecules/cm², with 12-month moving average
💡 when the line rises, traffic, factories and fuel burning are increasing — a rough sign of busier economic activity. When it falls, activity or emissions are lower. The gold dashed line smooths out the ups and downs to show the overall trend.

NO₂ Pollution Hotspots by Province

Mean tropospheric NO₂ column over the last 12 available months
💡 darker red = dirtier air from traffic and industry. Phnom Penh is normally the darkest; quiet, forested provinces stay pale.
Satellite grid: 0.25°; provincial value = mean of grid cells in each province. Border provinces can pick up transboundary pollution.

Monthly Rainfall & Anomalies ⓘ

Monthly rainfall (mm) vs long-term monthly average (dashed). Dark blue = wet anomaly (>125% of average) · amber = dry anomaly (<75%)
💡 each bar is one month of rain. Bars above the dashed line mean wetter than usual, below it drier than usual. Dark-blue bars = unusually wet (flood risk); amber bars = unusually dry (bad for rice and hydropower).

NDVI — Vegetation Health ⓘ

Monthly mean NDVI over the selected area vs historical average for the same calendar month
💡 higher = greener, healthier crops and forests. The line naturally rises in the rainy season and dips in the dry season; if it sits below the dashed line, plants are doing worse than normal for that time of year.

Vegetation Health by Province

Mean NDVI over the last 12 available months — red (stressed) to green (healthy)
💡 green = healthy vegetation, red = stressed or bare ground. Cities like Phnom Penh look red because of buildings, not crop failure.
Satellite grid: 0.1°; provincial value = mean of grid cells in each province.

Combined View — Standardized Anomalies (z-scores)

NO₂, rainfall and NDVI on a common scale: each month is expressed as standard deviations from its own calendar-month average, so the three indicators can be compared despite different units. Bars above zero = above seasonal norm.
💡 all three indicators on one common scale — bars above zero mean "higher than normal for that month", below zero "lower than normal". Reading tip: a long run of blue bars below zero (dry) followed by green bars below zero (stressed plants) = drought developing.

Risk Dashboard

🟢 Stable · 🟡 Watch · 🔴 High risk
Rule-based assessment computed from NBC · MEF · NIS · IMF · WB · ADB assessments; thresholds shown per row

Risk Radar

0 = low risk, 100 = high risk
Method: Each risk is assessed using a traffic-light scale of Low, Moderate (Watch), or High, based on rule-based thresholds applied to the latest available data. The thresholds are presented in the Vulnerability Table below. Each rating is converted into a score: Low = 15, Moderate = 55, and High = 90. The resulting scores are plotted on the radar chart. The thresholds are broadly aligned with NBC monitoring practices and IMF and World Bank conventions. For example, inflation above 4% is classified as Moderate (Watch), while inflation above 6% is classified as High. International reserves below three months of imports indicate High risk. These thresholds are indicative and should not be interpreted as official risk ratings.
